Treatment:

There are no cures for viral diseases such as mosaic once a plant is infected. Every effort should be made to prevent the disease from entering your garden. Below are some preventative measures:

1. Fungicides will NOT treat this viral disease.
2. Plant resistant varieties when available or purchase transplants from a reputable source.
3. Do NOT save seed from infected crops.
4. Spot treat with least-toxic, natural pest control products, such as Safer Soap, Bon-Neem, and diatomaceous earth, to reduce the number of disease-carrying insects.
5. Use Harvest-GuardÂ® row cover to keep insect pests off vulnerable crops/transplants.
6. Remove all perennial weeds within 100 yards of your garden plot using least-toxic herbicides.
7. Disinfect tools, stakes, ties, and pots with a solution of one part bleach to four parts water.
8. Avoid working in the garden during damp conditions, as viruses spread easily when plants are wet.
9. Avoid using tobacco around susceptible plants, as tobacco products may be infected.
10. Remove and destroy all infected plants. Do NOT compost them.
